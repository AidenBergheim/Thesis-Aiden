The minimum eigenvalue of the solution $P(t)$ is given by

$$\lambda_{min}(P(t)) = \min_{\boldsymbol{\nu} \in S^1} \underbrace{\int_{0}^{t} e^{-(t-s)} \cos^2(\gamma_{\boldsymbol{\nu}}(s)) \, ds}_{=:h_{\boldsymbol{\nu}}(0,t)}$$
where $S^1 \subset \mathbb{R}^2$  is a set defined as $S:=\{\boldsymbol{\nu} \in \mathbb{R}^2:\|\boldsymbol{\nu}\|=1\}$ and $\gamma_{\boldsymbol{\nu}}(s)$ denotes the angle measured in the counter clockwise direction from a unit vector $\boldsymbol{\nu} \in S^1$ to the vector $\bar{\boldsymbol{\varphi}}_i(t)$.
