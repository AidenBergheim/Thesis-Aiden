The following is bounded
$$ \frac{d\gamma_{\nu}(t)}{dt} $$
for $t\leq T_{c,1}$
# Proof
---
Lower bound:

- The centroid is always stationary, and the angular velocity of the agent is constant from **Proposition 1**
- Hence, the only way $\frac{d\gamma_{\nu}(t)}{dt}$ could be zero is if the agent is moving directly towards any of the targets.
- Since the agent is circling around all of the targets, this is never possible, and hence we must have $\frac{d\gamma_{\nu}(t)}{dt} > 0$
